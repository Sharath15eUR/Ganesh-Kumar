Process:
1. Represents a program in execution.
2. Consumes maximum time to stop.
3. Needs more time for work and conception.
4. Context switching takes maximum time.
5. Less effective in terms of communication.
6. Requires more resources.
7. Considered a heavyweight process.
8. If one process is blocked, it does not affect the operation of another process.

Thread:
1. A segment of a process responsible for executing specific tasks.
2. Consumes minimum time to stop.
3. Needs less time for work and conception.
4. Context switching takes minimum time.
5. Effective in terms of communication.
6. Requires fewer resources compared to processes.
7. Considered a lightweight process.
8. If one thread is blocked, it affects the execution of another thread within the same process.
